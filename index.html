Alexander Franks

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Statistics and Applied Probability at the University of California, Santa Barbara. My research interests include covariance estimation, multivariate analysis and high dimensional data, causal inference, missing data, and errors-in-variables models, missing data, and spatio-temporal methods. I work on many applied problems in computational biology.

I am also an active member of the XY Research group, which conducts research in sports statistics with a focus on player-tracking data.
